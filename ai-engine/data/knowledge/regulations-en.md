# Faculty of Electronic Engineering - Menoufia University Regulations

## Chapter One: General Provisions

### Article (1)
These regulations apply to all students enrolled at the Faculty of Electronic Engineering, Menoufia University, across all scientific departments and specializations.

### Article (2)
The faculty aims to prepare qualified engineers in the fields of electronic engineering, communications, computers, electrical power, and industrial and medical electronics.

### Article (3)
The Dean of the Faculty, the Faculty Council, and the scientific departments are responsible for overseeing the implementation of these regulations.

## Chapter Two: Study System

### Article (4): Duration of Study
The duration of study for the Bachelor's degree in Electronic Engineering is five academic years, divided into ten main semesters, in addition to a compulsory field training semester.

### Article (5): Semester System
The faculty follows a two-semester system (first semester - second semester) per academic year, with each semester lasting 16 academic weeks.

### Article (6): Credit Hours
The faculty adopts the credit hour system. The minimum for graduation is 160 credit hours for engineering programs. The maximum student load per semester is 22 credit hours. The minimum student load is 12 credit hours (15 credit hours for students with GPA above 3.0).

## Chapter Three: Enrollment and Registration

### Article (7): Academic Departments
The faculty comprises the following scientific departments:
1. Electronics and Communications Engineering
2. Computer Engineering and Systems
3. Electrical Power and Machines Engineering
4. Industrial Electronics and Automatic Control Engineering
5. Biomedical Engineering
6. Engineering Mathematics and Physics (Supporting Department)
7. Languages and Humanities (Supporting Department)

### Article (8): Admission Requirements
For admission to the faculty, a student must:
- Hold a General Secondary Education Certificate in Mathematics section or equivalent
- Meet the minimum admission score determined by the Coordination Office
- Pass the required medical examination
- No more than five years have passed since obtaining the secondary certificate

### Article (9): Course Registration
Students register for courses at the beginning of each semester during the first and second weeks. Students may add or drop courses during the third week according to the following controls:
- Academic advisor approval is required
- The study load must not exceed the maximum allowed (22 credit hours)
- Prerequisites for the requested courses must be fulfilled
- The minimum study load is 12 credit hours (15 credit hours for distinguished students with GPA above 3.0)

### Article (10): Add/Drop Periods
- Course registration: During the first and second weeks of the semester
- Adding courses: Only during the third week with academic advisor approval
- Dropping courses: Only during the third week
- No modifications are allowed after the third week ends

### Article (11): Postponement and Semester Withdrawal
A student may apply for postponement of enrollment or withdrawal from the semester for compelling reasons accepted by the faculty, during the first four weeks of the semester. A withdrawal semester is not counted within the maximum period of stay in the faculty.

### Article (12): Prerequisites
Students must fulfill the prerequisites for any course before registering for it. No course may be registered without fulfilling its prerequisites without the approval of the Faculty Dean.

## Chapter Four: Assessment and Examinations

### Article (13): Assessment System
Assessment grades are distributed as follows:
- Semester Work: 30% (periodic tests, research, assignments)
- Midterm Exam: 20%
- Final Exam: 50%

### Article (14): Grading System
Student grades are assessed according to the following scale:

| Grade | Percentage | Points |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Excellent (A) | 90% and above | 4.0 |
| Excellent Minus (A-) | 85% to less than 90% | 3.7 |
| Very Good (B+) | 80% to less than 85% | 3.3 |
| Very Good (B) | 75% to less than 80% | 3.0 |
| Good (B-) | 70% to less than 75% | 2.7 |
| Good (C+) | 65% to less than 70% | 2.3 |
| Good (C) | 60% to less than 65% | 2.0 |
| Good (C-) | 55% to less than 60% | 1.7 |
| Pass (D+) | 50% to less than 55% | 1.3 |
| Pass (D) | 45% to less than 50% | 1.0 |
| Fail (F) | Less than 45% | 0.0 |

**Note:** The minimum passing grade in any course is D (45% and above). A failing grade (F) is not calculated in the GPA when the course is retaken, but it appears in the academic record.

### Article (15): Cumulative GPA
The cumulative GPA is calculated by dividing the total grade points (course points × credit hours) by the total credit hours studied.

**Calculation Formula:**
Cumulative GPA = (Total Course Points) ÷ (Total Enrolled Credit Hours)

**Example:**
- Course Math101 (4 credits) with grade A (4.0) = 16 points
- Course CS101 (3 credits) with grade B+ (3.3) = 9.9 points
- Semester GPA = (16 + 9.9) ÷ (4 + 3) = 25.9 ÷ 7 = 3.7

**Minimum Required:** 2.0 out of 4.0 for graduation
**Academic Warning Minimum:** Below 2.0
**Department Transfer Minimum:** 2.5

## Chapter Five: Academic Warning and Dismissal

### Article (16): Academic Warning
An academic warning is issued to a student in the following cases:
- Cumulative GPA falls below 2.0 in any semester
- Student fails 50% or more of registered courses in a single semester
- Student misses final exams without an acceptable excuse

**Warning Procedures:**
- First warning: Official warning with academic advice
- Second warning: Warning with mandatory improvement plan with academic advisor
- Third warning: Final warning with referral to Student Affairs Committee

### Article (17): Final Dismissal
A student is permanently dismissed from the faculty in the following cases:
- Receiving three consecutive or non-consecutive academic warnings
- Cumulative GPA falls below 1.5 after the fourth semester
- Exceeding the maximum period of stay in the faculty, which equals twice the study duration (10 years)
- Proved cheating in examinations for the third time

## Chapter Six: Detailed Academic Warning

### Article (18): Warning Cases in Detail

**Warning due to Low GPA:**
- If cumulative GPA falls below 2.0, an immediate academic warning is issued
- The student must improve GPA to 2.0 or above within one semester
- If no improvement, a second warning is issued
- After the third warning, the student is dismissed from the faculty

**Warning due to Repeated Failure:**
- Failing 50% or more of enrolled courses in one semester = warning
- Failing a prerequisite course prevents registration for dependent courses

**Warning due to Absence:**
- Attendance below 75% = official warning
- Attendance below 60% = warning with guardian notification
- Attendance below 50% = exam ban and course failure

## Chapter Seven: Transfer Between Departments

### Article (19): Transfer Conditions
A student may transfer between different scientific departments in the faculty after completing the fourth semester, subject to the following conditions:
- The student's cumulative GPA must be at least 2.5
- Approval of both the transferring-from and transferring-to departments
- Availability of places in the target department
- Fulfillment of prerequisites for the new department

## Chapter Eight: Field Training

### Article (20): Field Training
Every student is required to complete compulsory field training for at least 8 weeks in a specialized company or institution. The student submits a training report evaluated by a specialized committee.

**Training Requirements:**
- Completion of at least 8 weeks at an accredited training entity
- Submission of a detailed training report
- Presentation of training before an evaluation committee
- Receiving a grade of "Pass" or above

## Chapter Nine: Graduation Project

### Article (21): Graduation Project
Every final-year student is required to submit a graduation project in their specialization. Grades are distributed as follows:
- Committee evaluation: 70%
- Supervisor evaluation: 20%
- Presentation and discussion: 10%

**Graduation Project Requirements:**
- Submit project proposal at the beginning of the final semester
- Assign a project supervisor
- Submit a final report
- Present the project before a committee of at least three members
- Receive a grade of "Pass" or above in the project

## Chapter Ten: Attendance and Absence

### Article (22): Attendance and Absence Policy

**Required Attendance Rates:**
- **Minimum 75%:** To be eligible for the final exam
- **50% and above:** To maintain course enrollment
- **Below 50%:** Prohibited from taking the exam and course failure

**Attendance Calculation:**
Attendance Rate = (Number of Lectures Attended ÷ Total Number of Lectures) × 100

**Attendance Warning System:**
- When attendance falls below 75%: Official warning from academic advisor
- When attendance falls below 65%: Official warning with guardian notification
- When attendance falls below 50%: Prohibited from taking the final exam

**Accepted Excuses:**
- Official sick leave approved by Student Affairs
- Compelling circumstances approved by the faculty
- Participation in official faculty events

**Exam Absence Procedures:**
- Absence from midterm exam: No grade for that exam
- Absence from final exam with excuse: Exam is retaken within two weeks
- Absence from final exam without excuse: Course failure recorded

## Chapter Eleven: Examinations

### Article (23): Examination Regulations

**Exam System:**
- Midterm examinations are held in the eighth week of each semester
- Final examinations are held in weeks 15 to 16
- Results are announced within two weeks after examinations end

**Final Exam Rules:**
- Must carry university ID card during the exam
- No electronic devices allowed unless specified for the exam
- Exam starts at the scheduled time and no student is accepted after 15 minutes from exam start
- In case of justified absence from final exam, the exam is retaken within two weeks

### Article (24): Cheating in Examinations
A student proven to have cheated in examinations is subject to the following penalties:
- **First time:** Prohibited from taking the exam in the course where caught and course failure
- **Second time:** Prohibited from taking the exam in the course where caught and course failure + official warning
- **Third time:** Permanent dismissal from the faculty

**Types of Cheating:**
- Using unauthorized notes or references
- Talking to another student during the exam
- Using a mobile phone or electronic device
- Copying answers from another student
- Any form of cheating determined by the faculty council

## Chapter Twelve: Course Retaking and Grade Improvement

### Article (25): Course Retaking
A student who fails any course must re-register for that course at the earliest opportunity. The retake grade is included in the cumulative GPA and the previous grade is not counted.

### Article (26): Grade Improvement
A student who received a Pass grade (D) in any course may re-register for the course to improve the grade, within the following two semesters. The new grade is included in the cumulative GPA.

**Conditions:**
- Grade improvement is allowed only once per course
- Grade improvement is not allowed for grades below D
- Improvement must be done within the two semesters following the original grade

### Article (27): Grades in Academic Record
- A failing grade (F) appears in the academic record but is not calculated in GPA when the course is retaken
- A retaken course grade appears as a note in the academic record

## Chapter Thirteen: Distinguished Students

### Article (28): Distinguished Students
Distinguished students with a cumulative GPA of 3.5 or above are honored at the end of each academic year and listed on the faculty's honor roll.

**Honor Designations:**
- **Honor Roll:** GPA 3.5 - 3.79
- **First Class Honors:** GPA 3.8 - 3.99
- **High Honors:** GPA 4.0

## Chapter Fourteen: Appeals

### Article (29): Right to Appeal
A student has the right to appeal any academic decision within two weeks from the date of the decision. The appeal is submitted in writing to the Faculty Dean.

**Appeal Procedures:**
1. Submit the appeal in writing within two weeks of the decision
2. The appeal is studied by a specialized academic committee
3. The final decision is issued within one month of submitting the appeal
4. The committee's decision is final

**Accepted Appeal Reasons:**
- Error in grade calculation
- Documented compelling circumstances
- Irregularities in exam procedures

## Chapter Fifteen: Miscellaneous Provisions

### Article (30): Guardian Rights
The student's guardian has the right to review their academic results and communicate with student affairs and the academic advisor to monitor their academic progress.

### Article (31): Graduation Requirements

**General Graduation Requirements (All Departments):**
1. Completion of at least 160 credit hours
2. Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0
3. No unresolved academic warnings
4. Completion of field training (minimum 8 weeks)
5. Submission of graduation project with grade of "Pass" or above
6. Fulfillment of all department-specific requirements

**Department-Specific Requirements:**
- **Electronics and Communications Engineering:** Graduation project course (4 credits)
- **Computer Engineering and Systems:** Graduation project course (4 credits) + additional lab hours
- **Electrical Power and Machines:** Graduation project course (4 credits) + additional lab hours
- **Industrial Electronics and Control:** Graduation project course (4 credits)
- **Biomedical Engineering:** Graduation project course (4 credits) + additional lab hours

### Article (32): Maximum Study Duration
The maximum study duration for remaining in the faculty is twice the original study duration (10 years). After this period, the student is permanently dismissed from the faculty.

### Article (33): Academic Advising

**Role of Academic Advisor:**
- Help students select appropriate courses
- Monitor academic progress
- Provide academic advice
- Resolve academic problems
- Plan for academic and professional future

**How to Contact Academic Advisor:**
- Book an appointment through the student portal
- Contact via email
- Visit the advisor's office during consultation hours

### Article (34): University Transfer Regulations
A student may transfer from another faculty to the Faculty of Electronic Engineering at Menoufia University subject to the following conditions:
- Fulfillment of admission requirements at the new faculty
- Approval of both the sending and receiving faculties
- Study for the remaining semesters
- Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5

## Chapter Sixteen: Academic Integrity and Conduct

### Article (35): Academic Conduct
Students must maintain proper academic conduct within the faculty campus. Penalties for any violation are applied according to faculty regulations.

### Article (36): Student Rights
Every student has the right to:
- Obtain clear information about courses and grades
- Consult the academic advisor
- Appeal any academic decision
- Maintain privacy of academic data
- Participate in student activities
