# Academic Guide - Faculty of Electronic Engineering, Menoufia University

## Introduction
Welcome to the Faculty of Electronic Engineering (FEE), Menoufia University. This academic guide provides you with all the necessary information for your academic journey.

## Academic Departments and Specializations

### 1. Electronics and Communications Engineering Department
The department graduates specialized engineers in electronics and communications. Specialization areas include:
- Electronic circuit design
- Digital and analog communication systems
- Wired and wireless communication networks
- Digital signal processing
- Radar and navigation systems
- Fiber optic technology

### 2. Computer Engineering and Systems Department
The department focuses on computer systems design and development:
- Software engineering
- Computer networks
- Database systems
- Artificial intelligence and machine learning
- Information security
- Embedded systems

### 3. Electrical Power and Machines Engineering Department
The department specializes in electrical power fields:
- Electrical power generation, transmission, and distribution
- Electrical machines
- Electrical protection systems
- Renewable energy sources
- Power control systems

### 4. Industrial Electronics and Automatic Control Engineering Department
The department focuses on automation and control:
- Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) systems
- Robotics
- Industrial process control systems
- Power electronics
- Industrial automation

### 5. Biomedical Engineering Department
The department bridges engineering and medicine:
- Medical devices
- Biomedical signal processing
- Medical imaging
- Bioengineering
- Health information systems

## Study Plan

### First Year - General Level
The first year includes general courses in mathematics, physics, programming, and English:
- Engineering Mathematics (1, 2)
- Engineering Physics (1, 2)
- Programming Fundamentals
- English Language
- Linear Algebra
- Engineering Chemistry
- Engineering Drawing
- Electronics Fundamentals
- Principles of Electrical Engineering

### Second Year - Pre-Specialization
Students begin studying introductory specialization courses:
- Differential Equations
- Advanced Engineering Mathematics
- Electric Circuits (1, 2)
- Electronics (1, 2)
- Advanced Programming
- Communication Principles
- Electromagnetic Field Theory
- Engineering Mechanics
- Numerical Analysis

### Third Year - Specialization Start
Students study specialized courses according to their chosen department.

### Fourth Year - Advanced Specialization
Advanced courses in the specialization field.

### Fifth Year - Graduation Project and Application
Includes applied courses, graduation project, and field training.

## Academic Advising

### The Role of the Academic Advisor
The academic advisor is a faculty member who guides students in:
- Selecting appropriate courses
- Monitoring academic progress
- Resolving academic problems
- Planning for academic and professional future

### Tips for Academic Success
1. Attend lectures regularly (attendance rate not less than 75%)
2. Organize your time between study and activities
3. Consult your academic advisor regularly
4. Participate in study groups
5. Use resources available at the faculty library
6. Submit assignments on time
7. Prepare well for examinations

## Student Services

### Faculty Library
The faculty library contains thousands of scientific references and specialized periodicals in all fields of electronic engineering. Working hours: 8 AM to 8 PM all week except Friday.

### Computer Labs
Computer labs equipped with the latest engineering software are available. Labs operate from 8 AM to 10 PM.

### Workshops
Equipped workshops for training students in practical skills in electronics, electrical, and mechanics.

### Student Activities
The faculty encourages student activities through:
- Student project teams
- Scientific competitions
- Student conferences
- Seminars and workshops
- Sports and cultural activities

## Study Schedules
Lectures are distributed across weekdays from Saturday to Thursday, with morning sessions from 8 AM to 3 PM. Each lecture lasts between one and two hours depending on the course nature.

## Examinations
Midterm examinations are held in the eighth week of each semester. Final examinations are held in weeks 15 to 16. Results are announced within two weeks after examinations end.

## Academic Values
The faculty adheres to the following academic values:
- Academic integrity and anti-cheating
- Respect for intellectual property rights
- Equal opportunities for all students
- Transparency in assessment
- Mutual respect between students and faculty members
